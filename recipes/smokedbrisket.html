Smoked Brisket

Description

This smoked brisket recipe has been a treasured family tradition, passed down through multiple generations, each adding their own touch while preserving its essence. It's more than just a recipe; it's a piece of our family history, shared at countless gatherings and celebrations. The process of smoking brisket, with its rich aromas and tender, flavorful meat, brings me back to those cherished times watching my grandfather work the grill. This recipe embodies the heart and soul of our family, making every bite a journey through our shared memories.

For meat lovers, smoked brisket is the quintessential experience, offering a melt-in-your-mouth tenderness and a smoky, savory flavor that is truly unparalleled. Yet, despite its impressive results, this recipe is perfect for beginners. With clear, easy-to-follow steps, even those new to grilling can achieve mouthwatering success. Whether it's a special occasion or a weekend barbecue, this smoked brisket is sure to impress and bring everyone together, creating new memories while honoring the old.

Ingredients

  • Wood chips
  • 1/4 cup paprika
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup ground cumin
  • 1/4 cup cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup chili powder
  • 1/4 cup garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup onion powder
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup freshly cracked black pepper
  • 10 pounds beef brisket, or more to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix all the spices together to create the rub.
  2. Apply the rub generously to the brisket, covering all sides.
  3. Wrap the brisket in plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
  4. Prepare the smoker with wood chips and heat to 225 degrees F (110 degrees C).
  5. Place the brisket in the smoker and cook for 10-12 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 195 degrees F (90 degrees C).
  6. Remove the brisket from the smoker and let it rest for at least 30 minutes before slicing.
